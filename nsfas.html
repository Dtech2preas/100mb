About NSFAS

The National Student Financial Aid Scheme (NSFAS) is a South African government initiative that provides financial assistance to eligible students at public universities and TVET colleges. Funded by the Department of Higher Education and Training, NSFAS covers tuition fees, accommodation, and other essential study-related costs for qualifying students.

2025 Application Dates

  • University Applications: Opened on 20 September 2024 and closed on 15 December 2024.
  • TVET College Applications:
    • Trimester 2: 12 May 2025 – 23 May 2025
    • Semester 2: 14 July 2025 – 31 July 2025
    • Trimester 3: 8 September 2025 – 19 September 2025

Note: All applications must be submitted online via the NSFAS website.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Must be a South African citizen or permanent resident.
  • Combined annual household income should not exceed R350,000.
  • For the NSFAS loan scheme, household income must be between R350,001 and R600,000.
  • Must be registered or intending to register at a public university or TVET college.
  • Applicants receiving SASSA grants automatically qualify.
  • First-time applicants must be pursuing their first undergraduate qualification.

For detailed eligibility information, refer to the NSFAS Loan Scheme Guidelines.

How to Apply

  1. Visit the NSFAS website.
  2. Create a myNSFAS account or log in if you already have one.
  3. Complete the online application form with accurate personal and academic details.
  4. Upload all required supporting documents, including:
    • Certified copy of your ID or birth certificate.
    • Proof of household income (e.g., payslips, affidavits).
    • Consent form signed by your parent/guardian.
  5. Submit your application before the closing date.
